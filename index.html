About

I am a research intern at the Ampere Laboratory at INSA Lyon, France, under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Bruno Allard. Simultaneously, I am pursuing my B. Tech degree at Galgotias College of Engineering and Technology, specializing in Electronics and Communication Engineering. Previously, I served as an intern at NMRC (Noida Metro Railway Corporation) in the signalling and telecommunication department.

I am currently volunteering at King’s College London, contributing to the research and development of Rectenna systems. My research interests primarily revolve around designing and fabricating Rectennas, Antennas, and RF rectifiers, as well as analyzing the RF properties of substrates used in the rectifiers. Additionally, I possess expertise in-circuit debugging and mathematical analysis, specifically in the context of RF energy harvesting from the ambient environment. My proficiency in programming languages such as Python and Matlab has proven instrumental in solving intricate circuit-related problems encountered during my research.

During my undergraduate studies, I initiated the establishment of 'Digital Dreamweaver,' a community at my college dedicated to fostering knowledge sharing and resource accessibility for fellow B.Tech students. The community, which currently boasts a membership of over 70 individuals, maintains comprehensive records of resources, achievements, and setbacks to aid future B.Tech aspirants.

Moreover, I harbour a keen interest in digital design and video editing, which has allowed me to explore my creative abilities, particularly in the realm of graphic design. Notably, I have contributed my skills as a graphic designer at both Lampros Tech and Dynamix Creations.

Looking for an opportunity to work in a challenging position combining my skills in RF Engineering, which provides professional development, interesting experiences and personal growth.
